Research

My research focuses on the intersection of environmental economics and macro finance, examining how policy mechanisms influence firm behavior, investment decisions, and the transition to a low-carbon economy. I am particularly interested in how carbon pricing mechanisms, such as carbon taxes and cap-and-trade systems, interact with financial markets and corporate strategies.


My current project investigates how carbon pricing mechanisms interact with firm dynamics and investment behavior, particularly in relation to green investment. I analyze whether carbon pricing policies incentivize or deter firms from investing in green technologies and how these effects are influenced by broader macroeconomic conditions, including monetary policy. By studying these interactions, my research aims to provide insights into the effectiveness of policy tools in driving sustainable investment and mitigating climate change.
